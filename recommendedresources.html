Recommended Resources


Reedsy Blog

The Reedsy Blog is one of the prominent free sources in the writing community. Within the blog, there are tips for everyone to improve in all areas such as: characters and publishing. If you would like to further your knowledge in honing your writer for creative pieces, here is a list of other writing websites by Reedsy Blog: The 50 Best Writing Websites of 2020



YouTube

A topic constantly in the limelight is the accessibility of information about anything and everything on the internet. YouTube is a well-known free resource for any topic you want. There are countless channels made by aspiring and professional bloggers, entrepreneurs, authors, editors--all of which offer advice based on their journey.




Daily Writing Tips

Daily Writing tips is a website which gives, as hinted by the name, daily advice with a focus on grammar and vocabulary. This is a great way to brush up on grmmar practces as each post is concise.


Zotero Bib

Zotero Bib is a free citation website which allows to quickly input citations in a bibliography for your projects, does not require an account or download (Zotero Bib) and has a simple layout. However, another alternative is Zotero,which is recommended if sources may need to be reused, shared or if you want create other bibliographies.


Your Teachers

One of the simplest ways that are often overlooked is asking your teachers for advice. Teachers are always willing to help, especially if is regarding difficulty in work.


Grammar Checker

Another trend is the use of grammar checkers, such as Grammarly, Hemingway Editor are some of the favourites. Grammar checkers, similar to citation generators, are not foolproof as they do sometimes gloss certain mistakes such as hymonyms. However, with advanced options such as checking the tone of your writing, they are amazing tools for writing in general.
